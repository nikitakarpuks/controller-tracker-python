Q: Why does accelerometer measure acceleration, not velocity?

A: Because force on the proof mass satisfies 
𝐹 = 𝑚𝑎 = 𝑘𝑥
, so displacement (measured via capacitance) is proportional to acceleration.

Q: What connects capacitance and displacement?

A: 𝐶 = 𝜀𝐴/𝑑
, and small displacement changes gap → differential capacitance ∝ displacement.

Q: Why do IMUs have bias if physics is correct?

A: Manufacturing imperfections, temperature changes, electronics offsets, and aging introduce constant errors.

Q: Why does IMU drift explode over time?

A: Bias integrates:
* velocity error grows linearly
* position error grows quadratically

Q: Why not use IMU alone?

A: It has no absolute reference → drift becomes huge over time.

Q: Why not use vision alone?

A: Too slow, fails under blur, occlusion, low texture.

Q: Why combine IMU + vision?

A:

IMU → fast, smooth motion

vision → corrects drift
Together → robust tracking

Q: What does IMU do in fusion?

A: Predicts motion continuously (high frequency).

Q: What does vision (or IR tracking) do?

A: Provides absolute pose measurements (low frequency).

Q: What is corrected during fusion?

A:
* position
* orientation
* IMU biases

Q: Do we modify IMU measurements directly?

A: No. We update the state estimate, not raw IMU data.

Q: What is PnP in controller tracking?

A: Estimating controller pose from known 3D LED positions and their 2D image projections.

Q: Is controller tracking SLAM?

A: No. It’s model-based pose estimation (PnP).

Q: Why use infrared LEDs?

A: Easy detection, robust to lighting, no need for natural features.

Q: Do IR LEDs work continuously?

A: Yes, typically continuously but modulated (pulsed) for robustness.

Q: Why does tracking fail outdoors?

A: Sunlight emits strong infrared → destroys signal-to-noise ratio.

Q: Will cameras always see controllers?

A: No — limited field of view, occlusions, fast motion.

Q: What happens when controller is not visible?

A: System falls back to IMU → drift starts accumulating.

Q: Why is IMU critical for VR?

A: Provides low-latency motion → prevents lag and motion sickness.

Q: What is SLAM doing in VR headset?

A: Tracking headset pose in the environment and maintaining a consistent map.

Q: Do we need IR points for SLAM?

A: No. SLAM uses natural image features.
